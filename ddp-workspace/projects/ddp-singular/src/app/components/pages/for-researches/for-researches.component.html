Uncovering Single Ventricle Etiology

Project Singular aims to identify the underlying genetic elements that contribute to the development of single ventricle heart defects and their related outcomes.

Through this initiative, we will:

  • Conduct whole genome sequencing on at least 5,000 patients and 200 immediate family members,
  • Create an annotated database,
  • Provide the analytical tools needed to assess the data, and
  • Connect with other related registries, where possible.

This genetic library will be an open resource for researchers while also ensuring the anonymity and privacy of all participants through a rigorous de-identification process.

Accessing Project Singular Data

Access to Project Singular data by researchers is intentionally inclusive.
We don’t know where the greatest discoveries will be made, and we aim to be as open as possible and strive for true democratization of data while protecting our participants’ safety and privacy.
