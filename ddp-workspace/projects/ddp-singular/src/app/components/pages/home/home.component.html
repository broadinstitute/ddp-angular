Here’s what we know:

5 of every 100,000 babies
are born with a single ventricle heart defect.
4 genes have been linked to
single ventricle heart defects.
Right now, there is NO cure
for single ventricle heart defects.

What is Project Singular?

Project Singular is a research study to learn more about single ventricle heart disease.

We don't know what causes single ventricle heart defects
or many of the associated health problems. If we understand the causes, researchers may be able to develop new and better treatments.

We hope that by looking at the genes of at least 5,000 patients and their immediate family members, we can make life-saving discoveries.

In order to reach our goal, we need your help.

Who can join?

For those who live in the USA or Canada:

Anyone who has ever been diagnosed with a
single ventricle heart defect, including transplant recipients, can participate.
Biological parents, siblings, or children of a person
with a single ventricle heart defect can participate.
We welcome people of all ages, genders, races, ethnicities, and abilities.
Living with single ventricle heart disease can feel powerless—but you have the power to fuel research and a brighter tomorrow. Let’s get started today.

Participating is Easy

Step 1

To enroll, set up an account on this website.

Complete a short survey and sign a consent.

Step 2

Provide your spit sample (we’ll send a returnable kit).

Patients will be asked to share their medical records.

Step 3

Check back here and on our social media pages to learn about progress and when there are more ways to help.

We are committed to protecting your privacy. Learn about how we are keeping your information safe, why we are asking for medical records, and more in our FAQs.
