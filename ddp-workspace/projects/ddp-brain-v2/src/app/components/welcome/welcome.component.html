Help transform our understanding of Brain Cancer

If you or your child have been diagnosed with a primary brain cancer, you can join a nationwide movement of patients and their families, doctors, and scientists by sharing your information and your voice.

Examples of primary brain cancers: glioblastoma, astrocytoma, oligodendroglioma, and medulloblastoma.

What’s this project about?

The Brain Cancer Project is part of Count Me In, a nonprofit organization that’s pursuing a new approach to cancer research - bringing patients and researchers together as partners to accelerate discoveries.

Patients with brain cancer are empowered to contribute to breakthroughs and increase the pace of biomedical research, no matter where they live. By discovering the genes and the variants that drive this disease and sharing this data, we hope insights can be gained to develop more effective therapies.

To learn more about Count Me In, our mission, and other projects and published research, visit our site.

What does participation look like?

• STEP 1 10-15 minutes

Provide Consent

This document is several pages, but take your time. Your progress will be automatically saved so you can come back to it later.

Preview the consent form

• STEP 2 5 minutes

Tell us where you’ve been treated

We ask where patients have been treated, so that we can request medical records, and if applicable, archived tumor tissue.

Preview the medical release

• STEP 3 10 minutes

Respond to simple surveys

In these surveys, we ask questions about your experience with brain cancer, like your brain tumor type, when you were diagnosed, and the types of treatments you’ve had.

Preview the surveys

• STEP 4 10 minutes

Provide a saliva and/or blood sample

We will send you a kit to collect a sample of your saliva and/or blood. We will compare the normal DNA in your saliva or blood to your tumor DNA.
