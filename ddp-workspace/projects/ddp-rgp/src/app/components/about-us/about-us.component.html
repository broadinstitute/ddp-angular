Our Story

We created the Rare Genomes Project to enable patients with undiagnosed, suspected genetic conditions to participate in genomic research regardless of where they live, to engage participants as partners in the research process, and to turn genomic data into clinically meaningful answers for families.

Improving our understanding of the rarest genetic conditions requires combining the experiences of patients and families directly with genomic research. By reaching out nationwide and empowering the rare and undiagnosed community to donate a sample and their story, we hope to better understand the genetic basis of all undiagnosed diseases.

The Broad Institute

The Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard is a non-profit, academic research institution based in Cambridge, Massachusetts, focused on using genomic data to better understand human health and disease. The Broad is home to sequencing facilities that can produce and analyze genetic data at the rate of an entire genome every 12 minutes—more systematically and at a larger scale than any other facility. We have developed new advances in genome sequencing and analysis technology that have allowed us to find the genetic changes at the root of the diseases of thousands of patients. The Broad’s research philosophy is centered on large-scale collaborative research and open data sharing.

Despite these exciting improvements in diagnosis, however, the vast majority of rare disease patients still remain undiagnosed. The Broad is committed to changing this for all rare disease patients, and we are sharing our resources with the research community at large to make sure that we are advancing rare disease research worldwide. For more information about the Broad Institute, go to broadinstitute.org.
